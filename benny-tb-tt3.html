Tana Talk 3

2018

Top 3 Songs

  • Intro: Babs
  • '97 Hov

9.1

Genre: Rap

Label: Griselda Records

Review

Benny The Butcher's "Tana Talk 3" is a gritty and raw testament to the essence of hardcore hip-hop. From start to finish, the Buffalo rapper delivers hard-hitting bars over soulful and atmospheric production, painting vivid pictures of street life and survival in the face of adversity. With its cinematic storytelling and uncompromising lyricism, "Tana Talk 3" stands as a testament to Benny's lyrical prowess and his unwavering dedication to his craft.

The album wastes no time in grabbing the listener's attention, kicking off with the haunting "Intro: Babs." Over a soulful sample and a hypnotic beat, Benny sets the stage for the lyrical onslaught that follows, setting the tone for the rest of the project. Tracks like "Goodnight" and "Scarface vs. Sosa, Pt. 2" showcase Benny's razor-sharp wit and his ability to paint vivid pictures with his words, while tracks like "Broken Bottles" and "51" delve into themes of struggle, resilience, and triumph in the face of adversity.

One of the standout tracks on the album is "Rubber Bands & Weight," which features guest appearances from Conway The Machine and Westside Gunn. Over a haunting piano loop and gritty drums, the trio of Buffalo rappers trade blistering bars, showcasing their undeniable chemistry and their shared dedication to the art of lyricism. It's a track that demands repeated listens, as you catch new nuances with each playthrough.

Throughout "Tana Talk 3," Benny proves himself to be a master storyteller, weaving together intricate narratives that are as captivating as they are compelling. Tracks like "97 Hov" and "Who Are You" offer glimpses into Benny's past and his come-up in the rap game, while tracks like "Echo Long" and "Joe Pesci 38" showcase his ability to reflect on the challenges and triumphs of his journey with wisdom and introspection.

The production on "Tana Talk 3" is equally impressive, with beats that range from soulful and atmospheric to gritty and hard-hitting. Producers like Daringer and The Alchemist provide the perfect backdrop for Benny's gritty lyricism, with their sample-heavy production adding depth and texture to the album's sound. Tracks like "Rick" and "Fast Eddie" feature beats that are as cinematic as they are menacing, adding to the album's overall sense of urgency and intensity.

In conclusion, "Tana Talk 3" is a tour de force of hardcore hip-hop, showcasing Benny The Butcher at the top of his game. With its gritty lyricism, cinematic storytelling, and atmospheric production, the album stands as a testament to Benny's talent and his unwavering dedication to his craft. Whether you're a longtime fan of hardcore hip-hop or a newcomer to Benny's music, "Tana Talk 3" is an essential addition to any hip-hop lover's collection, offering a raw and unfiltered glimpse into the mind of one of rap's most exciting talents.
